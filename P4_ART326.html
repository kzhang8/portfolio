Design Using Given/Found Data

1. Identify an area of personal interest.2. Conduct research to accumulate data related to your area of interest.3. Organize found data into distinct categories/groups and then try to identify relationships, patterns as well as possible layering and separation options. 4. Sketch by hand to visualize ideas for composition and size relationships.3. After following the above steps, only then move to the screen and start designing using the software of your preference.4. Utilize typography, color and form to create compelling graphics and visuals in order to communicate your given/found data in a way that engages the viewer in the most efficient way.5. Test print/preview/prototype to get a sense of the size and composition. Always try to incorporate test printing, previewing and prototyping into your process.6. Iterate: design, test print/preview/prototype, review, revise. Repeat.

Exercise: Wordless Diagrams

Create a set of graphic instructions or wordless diagrams for a simple everyday task such as How to Make a PBJ Sandwich, How to Iron an Oxford Shirt, How to Mow a Lawn, etc. Consider the kinds of details and nuance that can communicate in about four to eight steps. Final Group Result

You will work in four groups of four people researching and generating data for a public space. You will observe, record phenomenas and study the environment of this public space using the AEIOU framework and generate a set of data about your chosen space. Then you will come together as a team and after organizing and filtering all of your findings, you will design a piece that reflects on the data you have gathered. Look for patterns, phenomena, personas etc. Make sure you tell us a story about your subject. Process- Part 1: Split into four teams of four.Part 2: Observe, record and study the chosen public space. Part 3: Design a piece that reflects on the data that you have gathered.

Designing using Own/Personal Data
